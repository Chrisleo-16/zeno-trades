Liquidity sweeps
Price hunts stops above equal highs or below equal lows, then reverses. Look for this just before price enters your zone. A sweep + zone = very high probability.
BOS / CHoCH
Break of Structure confirms trend continuation. Change of Character (CHoCH) signals a potential reversal. On 4H/1H it filters direction. On 5M it triggers entry.
Fair Value Gaps (FVG)
A 3-candle imbalance where price moves too fast to fill orders. When an FVG overlaps your zone, it creates a magnet — institutions tend to fill it before continuation.
Kill zones (sessions)
London open (8–10am GMT) and NY open (7–10am GMT) are when big money moves. Trades taken outside kill zones on GU, GJ, EU, XAUUSD, US100 have lower probability.
Fibonacci (61.8–78.6%)
Draw from the last significant swing high to low (or reverse). When the 61.8 or 78.6 level sits inside your S&D zone, the zone is stronger. The 50% level is the premium/discount separator.
Nested zones (HTF + LTF)
A 1H demand zone inside a Daily demand zone = stacked institutional interest. This is one of the highest probability setups in your system.
Do not enter below 5/12 score
Fewer than 5 confluences means the setup is unconfirmed. Mark it on your chart and wait — don't chase.
Never trade against HTF bias
If Daily is bearish, skip all demand zone trades no matter how clean the LTF setup looks.
No entry outside kill zones
GU, GJ, EU, XAUUSD, US100 all move with London and NY sessions. Entries outside these windows have lower follow-through and wider spreads.
SL must be behind the full zone
Never put stop loss inside the zone. Place it 2–5 pips beyond the far edge of the zone body.
Minimum RR = 1:2
If the next HTF level doesn't give you at least 1:2, skip the trade. This protects your edge over time.
One trade per session max (while building)
Until this system is proven in your journal, take max one trade per session to protect your account during the learning phase.
